About Us

DJS Arya is the canister satellite team of D. J. Sanghvi College of Engineering. Our team builds a fully functional miniature satellite integrated with space technology that we present each year at the annual CanSat competition conducted by the American Astronomical Society (AAS) in collaboration with NASA held in the U.S.A.

Our team's journey to enter one of the biggest space related competitions in the world is a unique one. Initially, the idea to build a CanSat arose as a final year project of a few space enthusiast students from the EXTC department. After getting to know about the competition, they decided to take the plunge and participate in it. But, little did they know that this would soon transform into something much more than what they had planned. What started as a team of few, grew into a team of many dedicated and hardworking students, who were determined to make it big in the competition. After this there was no looking back, and the team has only soared to greater heights since.

Departments

The Coding department makes the graphical user interface (GUI) displaying telemetry data,
which is sent from the payload while it descends.

The mechanical department of the team looks after the designing aspect of the CanSat and
ensures proper placements of the electronic components in the CanSat.

The electronics department makes sure that the correct electronic components are chosen.
These help to measure various parameters and transmit them to the ground station.

The Marketing department oversees the social media, the sponsorship and other
management aspects of the team.

Our Achievements

Although we are one of the newer teams from our college, it hasn’t stopped us from excelling in the competition at the National and International level. Right from presenting our idea to the judges at an international level to testing our satellite on the beach, our team has worked together to ensure we come out at the top. With our imagination deployed at every step of the journey, we hope to always push our boundaries because the sky’s the limit.In the 2020 competition, we finished 9th overall in the world and 3rd in India. We also finished 2nd in the Critical Design Review (CDR) round. We most recently finished 2nd all over the world in our Preliminary Design Review (PDR) round in the 2021 edition of the competition.
Our faculty members of Dwarkadas J. Sanghvi College of Engineering have been our mentors right from the beginning and helped us participate and achieve great heights in the competition: Dr. Hari Vasudevan, Dr. Ashish Daptardar, Dr. Manali Godse, Prof. Amit Deshmukh, Ph.D., Prof. Tushar Sawant and Prof. Aarti Ambekar for their constant and consistent support which helped us along the way.
